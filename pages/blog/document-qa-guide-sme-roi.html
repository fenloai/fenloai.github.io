What Is Document Q&A?

A document Q&A system lets you ask plain-English questions about your company's documents and get specific answers back. Instead of searching for keywords and then reading through results, you ask something like "What's the warranty period in our standard vendor contract?" and get the actual answer, with a citation pointing to the source.

This isn't the same as basic document search. Traditional search tools match keywords. Document Q&A systems understand meaning. Ask about "refund policies" and they'll find content about "return procedures" or "money-back guarantees" even if those exact words weren't in your query.

When Document Q&A Might NOT Be Right for You

Here's where we differ from most AI vendors: we'll tell you when this isn't the right solution. Automation amplifies what you already have. If your foundation isn't solid, AI won't fix that.

Skip document Q&A if:

  • You have very few documents (under ~50 key documents that people regularly reference)
  • Your documents are already well-organized and easily searchable
  • Most questions require human judgment, not information retrieval
  • Your team is very small (1-3 people who already know where everything is)
  • Your documents are severely outdated or poorly maintained
Honest Assessment

We've told clients "no" when document Q&A wasn't the right fit. If your documents are a mess, you might need organization before AI. The technology works best when it's amplifying already-decent knowledge management, not replacing it entirely.

Consider document Q&A if:

  • The same questions get asked repeatedly across your team
  • New employees take months to "learn where everything is"
  • Audits or compliance reviews require quick document searches
  • Staff regularly complain about finding information
  • You're scaling and can't keep relying on people's memory
  • Key knowledge lives in the heads of a few people who might leave
