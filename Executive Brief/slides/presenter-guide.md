# Executive AI Briefing — Presenter Guide

A practical companion for delivering the 90-minute executive AI briefing. This is not a script — it's a navigation tool.

---

## Before You Begin

### Pre-Session Checklist

- [ ] Research their company (website, LinkedIn, recent news)
- [ ] Identify their industry's common AI use cases
- [ ] Test the slide deck in presentation mode
- [ ] Have notepad ready for parking lot items
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 industry-specific examples as backup
- [ ] Calculator app open for the interactive anchor

### Room Setup

- Position yourself where you can see their reactions
- Keep slides visible but not dominant — this is a conversation
- Have water available (for you and them)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Guide

### OPENING (Slides 1-4) — 10 minutes

---

#### Slide 1: Title
**Duration:** 30 seconds

**What to say:**
> "Thank you for making time for this. Over the next 90 minutes, I want to give you something you can actually use — a mental framework for thinking about AI that cuts through the noise."

**Transition:** Move quickly — this is just the opener.

---

#### Slide 2: Monday Morning, Two Companies
**Duration:** 3 minutes

**Set the scene:**
> "Imagine two companies. Same size, same industry, same Monday morning. But very different days."

*Walk through the table row by row. Don't rush — let each contrast land.*

**Key contrasts to emphasize:**
- Customer emails: "One team starts the day triaging. The other starts the day responding."
- Quote turnaround: "48 hours vs 4 hours. That's the difference between winning the deal and losing it to someone faster."
- Document processing: "15 hours a week — that's almost half a person just on data entry."

**The message:**
> "The difference isn't budget or headcount. It's knowing which AI to use where. That's what we're going to cover today."

**What you're doing:** Making it concrete and relatable. They should see themselves in Company A.

**Watch for:** Which rows they react to most — that signals their pain points.

---

#### Slide 3: Three Shifts That Changed Everything
**Duration:** 4 minutes

**Walk through each shift:**

1. **Cost:** "Five years ago, this was Fortune 500 only. Now a small business can access the same technology for a fraction of the cost."

2. **Accessibility:** "You don't need a PhD or a data science team. There are plug-and-play tools for most common use cases."

3. **Expectation:** "This is the one that matters most. Your customers expect it. Your employees expect it. It's becoming table stakes."

**The message (read the quote):**
> "This isn't about being first. It's about not being last."

**Watch for:** Questions about their competitors. Park them if needed.

---

#### Slide 4: The AI Iceberg
**Duration:** 3 minutes

**This is your anchor visual — you'll return to it throughout.**

> "This image captures what we're going to explore today. Above the waterline is what everyone talks about — ChatGPT, image generators, the trending stuff. Below the waterline is what's been quietly driving value for years."

**Point to each section as you describe it.**

**Checkpoint question:**
> "Does this framing match your experience? Or is it surprising?"

**What you're checking:** Whether they've bought in to the core premise.

---

### BLOCK 1: The AI Landscape (Slides 5-8) — 18 minutes

---

#### Slide 5: Section Divider
**Duration:** 15 seconds

> "Let's start by understanding what we're actually talking about when we say 'AI.'"

---

#### Slide 6: AI = Machines That Learn From Data
**Duration:** 3 minutes

**Keep this simple:**

> "The simplest way to think about it: Traditional software follows rules humans write. AI learns from examples and applies what it learned to new situations."

**The key insight:**
> "Different types of AI learn different things from different types of data. That's why there isn't one AI — there's a family of them."

---

#### Slide 7: Four Types of AI
**Duration:** 10 minutes

**This is a core teaching slide. Take your time.**

Walk through each card:

**Predictive AI:**
> "This is AI that forecasts the future. Netflix recommendations, fraud detection, demand forecasting. It's been in production for 20+ years. Very mature, very proven."

**Perception AI:**
> "This is AI that sees, hears, and reads. Face unlock on your phone, Zoom transcription, spam filters. Also very mature."

**Generative AI:**
> "This is the new one — the one everyone's talking about. ChatGPT, Midjourney, Copilot. It creates content. Medium maturity — 2-3 years, improving rapidly."

**Physical AI:**
> "This is AI that moves and acts in the real world. Warehouse robots, self-driving features, process automation. Requires significant investment."

**Checkpoint question:**
> "Quick gut check: which of these four feels most relevant to your business right now?"

**What you're checking:** Whether they understand the distinctions. Their answer will guide the rest of the session.

---

#### Slide 8: How They Work Together
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**Show that AI is rarely one type:**

> "The most powerful solutions combine multiple AI types. Let me show you two examples..."

Walk through the customer service and document workflow examples.

**The point:**
> "When you hear 'AI solution,' it's usually not one thing — it's a combination. That's also where off-the-shelf tools start to fall short and custom solutions make more sense."

---

### BLOCK 2: AI Capabilities (Slides 9-12) — 17 minutes

---

#### Slide 9: Section Divider
**Duration:** 15 seconds

> "Now that you know the AI types, let's make it practical. How do you match AI to your actual problems?"

---

#### Slide 10: Capability Framework
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**Six capabilities — keep it memorable:**

> "AI can do six things for business: See, Hear, Read, Predict, Create, and Act. Each maps to a type of AI."

Walk through quickly. This is a reference framework, not deep teaching.

---

#### Slide 11: Matching Problems to AI
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**This is where it clicks for them:**

> "Here's how to use this. Start with your problem, then find the capability you need."

Read through 2-3 examples, then ask:

> "Any of these sound like problems you're dealing with?"

**What you're doing:** Priming them for the interactive exercise after the break.

---

#### Slide 12: What AI Cannot Do
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**Be honest here — it builds trust:**

> "Now let me be honest about what AI can't do. This is important because the vendors won't tell you this."

Walk through the limitations table. Emphasize:

> "Garbage in, garbage out. AI has no judgment. It doesn't actually understand anything — it's pattern matching."

**Checkpoint question:**
> "Any of these capabilities feel foreign or unclear before we move on?"

---

### BREAK — 5 minutes

> "Let's take a quick 5-minute break. When we come back, I want to talk specifically about your business."

**What to do during the break:**
- Review your notes on what they've said so far
- Prepare to connect their situation to the framework
- Have the calculator ready for the interactive exercise

---

### BLOCK 3: Business Impact (Slides 13-19) — 18 minutes

---

#### Slide 13: Section Divider
**Duration:** 15 seconds

**Checkpoint question after break:**
> "Before we dive in — anything from the first half you want me to clarify?"

---

#### Slide 14: Let's Make This About You — THE ANCHOR
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**This is your most important slide. Be fully present.**

> "Before we look at frameworks and case studies, I want to make sure this is relevant to you — not just theory."

**Ask the question. Then stop talking:**
> "What's the one process that eats the most time for the least value? The thing your best people complain about."

**Wait for their answer. Write it down.**

Then do the math together:
- Hours per week × People × Hourly cost × 52 weeks

> "So this one problem is costing you roughly $X per year. And that's before we count opportunity cost."

**The pivot:**
> "Keep that number in mind. As we go through the case studies, think about what cutting that in half would mean."

**If they struggle:** Use the prompts on the slide. Most common answers:
- Data entry / manual processing
- Searching for information
- Answering repetitive questions
- Report generation

---

#### Slide 15: Five Ways AI Creates Value
**Duration:** 3 minutes

**Quick framework — don't linger:**

> "AI creates value in five ways: Cost reduction, revenue growth, risk mitigation, speed, and experience."

Point to the table showing which AI types drive which value.

> "Based on what you just told me about [their problem], we're probably looking at [cost/speed] as the primary value driver."

---

#### Slides 16-18: Case Studies
**Duration:** 5 minutes total (90 seconds each)

**Stitch Fix:** Predictive + Perception
> "They're using AI to predict what customers want before they know they want it. 80% of revenue driven by algorithms."

**Klarna:** Conversational AI
> "They replaced the equivalent of 700 agents with AI that resolves issues in 2 minutes instead of 11."

**JPMorgan:** Document AI
> "360,000 hours of lawyer time — now done in seconds."

**After the third case:**
> "Each story matched the right type of AI to a specific problem. These aren't startups — they're established companies with verifiable results."

**Checkpoint question:**
> "Any of those examples spark ideas about your situation?"

---

#### Slide 19: AI + Humans
**Duration:** 3 minutes

**Preempt the "will AI take jobs" concern:**

> "I want to address something that might be on your mind. The question isn't AI vs humans. It's how we combine them."

Walk through the two columns.

**Key message:**
> "The best implementations augment human capability. Your people do what humans are good at. AI handles the rest."

---

### BLOCK 4: Getting Started (Slides 20-23) — 17 minutes

---

#### Slide 20: Section Divider
**Duration:** 15 seconds

> "Let's get practical. What does it actually take to get started?"

---

#### Slide 21: Investment Framework
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**Demystify cost:**

> "Most people think AI requires a massive investment. It doesn't."

Walk through the ladder:
- Learn: Free
- Experiment: $0-500/month
- First Win: Under $1K
- Custom: $2K-10K
- Scale: $10K+

> "You can get a working solution that solves a real problem for less than you'd spend on a single hire."

---

#### Slide 22: FIT Assessment
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**Give them a decision framework:**

> "Here's how I'd evaluate any AI opportunity: FIT."

Walk through each letter:
- **F**easibility: Do we have the data? Is the tech mature?
- **I**mpact: Will this meaningfully move a metric?
- **T**imeframe: Can we see results reasonably soon?

**Reference their problem:**
> "For what you described — [their problem] — it sounds like [Feasibility: yes/no], [Impact: high/medium], [Timeframe: quick win/strategic bet]."

---

#### Slide 23: Start Monday
**Duration:** 5 minutes

**Make it concrete:**

> "Based on what we discussed, here's what I'd do if I were you..."

**Personalize this based on their situation:**
- If they identified a clear problem: recommend a specific tool to try
- If they're unsure: recommend the data audit
- If they're skeptical: recommend talking to their team first

---

### CLOSING (Slides 24-25) — 5 minutes

---

#### Slide 24: Three Takeaways
**Duration:** 3 minutes

**Reinforce the core messages:**

> "If you remember nothing else from today..."

Read each takeaway. Return to the iceberg visual mentally.

**The provocative question — deliver slowly:**
> "What would your team do with 10 extra hours per week?"

*Let it sit. Don't answer for them.*

---

#### Slide 25: Where Do You Go From Here
**Duration:** 2 minutes

**Three paths — present neutrally:**

1. Explore on your own
2. Go deeper on a topic
3. Talk through a specific challenge

**Closing thought:**
> "The goal today wasn't to sell you on AI. It was to give you a framework for thinking clearly about it. Now you have that framework."

**End warmly:**
> "Thank you for your time. I'm happy to answer any final questions, or we can follow up later if something comes to mind."

## Facilitation Tools

### The Parking Lot

When they ask something that would derail the flow:

> "That's a great question. Let me write it down so we don't forget, and I'll make sure we come back to it."

**Check the parking lot:**
- During transitions between blocks
- After the break
- At the end of the session

### Checkpoints

| After | Question |
|-------|----------|
| Slide 4 (Iceberg) | "Does this framing match your experience? Or surprising?" |
| Slide 7 (Four Types) | "Which of these four feels most relevant to your business?" |
| Slide 11 (Matching) | "Any of these sound like problems you're dealing with?" |
| After Break | "Anything from the first half you want me to clarify?" |
| Slide 18 (Cases) | "Any of those examples spark ideas about your situation?" |
| Slide 24 (Takeaways) | "What's the one thing that's stuck with you most?" |

### Signs of Confusion

Watch for:
- Furrowed brow, looking away
- Generic responses ("Yeah, makes sense...")
- Checking phone or watch
- Questions that suggest they missed something earlier

**If you see these:**
- Don't push forward — circle back
- Use a different example
- Ask what's unclear
- It's okay to skip ahead if a section isn't resonating

### Common Objections

| They Say | You Respond |
|----------|-------------|
| "We're too small for AI" | "Small companies actually move faster. And you can start for under $1K." |
| "Our data is a mess" | "Everyone's is. You don't need perfect data to start. Let's identify what you do have." |
| "I don't trust AI" | "Healthy skepticism is good. That's why we talked about limitations. The key is human oversight." |
| "My team won't adopt it" | "Change management is real. Start with the pain point they complain about most." |
| "Isn't this just hype?" | "The generative stuff is new. But predictive and perception AI have been delivering ROI for 20+ years." |

## Energy Management

| Block | Energy Level | Technique |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| Opening | High | Surprising hook, direct eye contact |
| Block 1 | Medium | Conceptual, check understanding frequently |
| Block 2 | High | Their business — make it personal |
| Break | Reset | Informal chat, let them process |
| Block 3 | Medium-High | Stories create connection |
| Block 4 | Medium | Practical, reference their situation |
| Closing | High | Specific, provocative question |
