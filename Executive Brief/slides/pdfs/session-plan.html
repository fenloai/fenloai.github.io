Executive AI Briefing — Session Plan

Executive AI Briefing — 90-Minute Session Plan

Session Goal

Create a lasting, comprehensive understanding of AI that transforms how business leaders think about the technology — moving from confusion/hype to clarity and actionable insight across the FULL spectrum of AI capabilities.

Session Philosophy

Core Principle: “The Complete Picture”

AI is not just ChatGPT. We cover the full landscape — from machine learning and predictive analytics to computer vision, robotics, speech recognition, and yes, generative AI. Executives leave understanding the entire toolkit, not just the trending tool.

Three Pillars: 1. Breadth — Understanding the full AI landscape, not just LLMs 2. Depth — How each type of AI creates business value 3. Judgment — Ability to match the right AI to the right problem

Session Structure Overview

Block Duration Focus Energy Level
Opening 10 min Hook + Why Now High
Block 1 18 min The AI Landscape Medium-High
Block 2 17 min AI Capabilities + Their Business High
Break 5 min Breather —
Block 3 18 min Business Impact (Anchor Story + Cases) Medium-High
Block 4 17 min Getting Started (Data, Investment, Action) Medium
Closing 5 min Key Takeaways + Call to Action High

Total: 90 minutes

Detailed Session Flow

🎬 OPENING (10 minutes)

Goal: Capture attention, create urgency, establish AI is broader than they think

0:00–0:03 — The Hook (3 min)

Slide: “The $4.4 Trillion Blind Spot”

Open with a striking fact: - “McKinsey estimates AI could add $4.4 trillion in value to the global economy annually.” - “But here’s what most people miss…” - “Only about 15% of that comes from ChatGPT and generative AI.” - Pause for effect - “So where’s the other 85%?”

Reveal: The AI everyone’s talking about is the smallest piece of the pie. The real value — the boring, proven, already-working AI — is invisible because nobody’s writing headlines about it.

Key Message: “Today, I’m going to show you what almost everyone overlooks. By the end, you’ll see opportunities your competitors are missing.”

Slide: “Three Shifts That Changed Everything”

Why this moment is different:

Shift What Changed Business Impact

Accessibility No-code tools, APIs, plug-and-play No PhD required to implement
Expectation Customers and employees expect AI Table stakes, not competitive advantage

  • 72% of executives say AI is a top priority (Deloitte 2024)
  • Your competitors are exploring this right now
  • The cost of waiting: falling behind on efficiency, customer experience, decision speed

Message: “This isn’t about being first. It’s about not being last.”

🧠 BLOCK 1: The AI Landscape (18 minutes)

Goal: Build comprehensive understanding of AI as a family of technologies

0:10–0:13 — What AI Actually Is (3 min)

Slide: “AI = Machines That Learn From Data”

The Simplest Explanation: - Traditional software: IF this THEN that (humans write every rule) - AI: Learn from examples → Find patterns → Apply to new situations

Key insight: Different types of AI learn different things from different types of data.

0:13–0:23 — The Four Core AI Types (10 min)

Slide: “Four Types of AI That Drive Business Value”

Consolidated from six to four for clarity and retention

1. PREDICTIVE AI — “AI that sees the future” Combines: Machine Learning, Analytics, Optimization

What It Does Examples You Know
Forecasts outcomes Netflix recommendations
Scores probability Credit card fraud alerts
Optimizes decisions Airline ticket pricing

Business use: Demand forecasting, churn prediction, lead scoring, predictive maintenance

Maturity: Very High — 20+ years in production

2. PERCEPTION AI — “AI that sees, hears, and reads” Combines: Computer Vision, Speech AI, NLP

What It Does Examples You Know
Interprets images/video Face unlock on phone
Transcribes speech Zoom meeting transcription
Understands text Gmail spam filter

Business use: Quality inspection, document processing, call center analytics, contract review

Maturity: High — 10+ years in production

3. GENERATIVE AI — “AI that creates” The new kid on the block

What It Does Examples You Know
Writes text ChatGPT, Claude
Creates images Midjourney, DALL-E
Generates code GitHub Copilot

Business use: Content creation, first drafts, code assistance, brainstorming

Maturity: Medium — 2-3 years, rapidly improving

4. PHYSICAL AI — “AI that moves and acts” Combines: Robotics, Autonomous Systems, RPA

What It Does Examples You Know
Controls machines Warehouse robots
Navigates space Self-driving features
Automates processes Automated workflows

Business use: Manufacturing, logistics, process automation

Maturity: Medium — Requires significant investment

0:23–0:25 — How They Work Together (2 min)

Slide: “AI Systems Combine Multiple Types”

Example: Modern Customer Service 1. Perception AI — Transcribes the call, understands intent 2. Predictive AI — Identifies best resolution, routes appropriately 3. Generative AI — Drafts the response or surfaces relevant knowledge

Example: Intelligent Document Workflow 1. Perception AI — Reads and extracts data from incoming documents 2. Predictive AI — Categorizes, flags exceptions, routes to right person 3. Generative AI — Summarizes key points, answers questions about content

Message: “The most powerful solutions combine multiple AI types. That’s also where custom solutions start to make more sense than off-the-shelf tools.”

0:25–0:28 — The Maturity Spectrum (3 min)

Slide: “Not All AI Is Created Equal”

Category Risk Level Examples Your Approach
Proven (10+ years) Low Fraud detection, recommendations, spam filters Deploy confidently
Established (5-10 years) Low-Medium Vision QC, transcription, chatbots Pilot and scale
Maturing (2-5 years) Medium Generative AI, autonomous systems Experiment carefully
Emerging (<2 years) Higher AGI research, advanced reasoning Watch and wait

Key insight: “Don’t confuse ‘new to you’ with ‘new to the world.’ Most valuable AI is boring and proven.”

Turn the conversation to their specific situation.

Facilitator asks directly: - “What’s the most time-consuming repetitive task in your business right now?” - “Where do you feel like you’re leaving money on the table?” - “What keeps you up at night operationally?”

As they share, guide them to the AI type: - Listen to their problem - Reflect back: “So it sounds like you need to [predict/see/create/automate]…” - Identify the AI type together - Briefly discuss what a solution might look like

Example prompts if they need help getting started:

0:40–0:45 — Understanding Limitations (5 min)

Slide: “What AI Cannot Do — Honest Truths”

AI Type Key Limitation Implication
Predictive Only as good as historical data Can’t predict black swans or paradigm shifts
Perception Struggles with edge cases Needs human review for exceptions
Generative Hallucinations — confidently wrong Must verify all facts and outputs
Physical Expensive, inflexible Best for controlled, repetitive environments

Universal Truths: - Garbage in = garbage out — Data quality determines AI quality - AI has no judgment — Can’t weigh ethical tradeoffs - AI doesn’t understand — Pattern matching, not comprehension - Security matters — AI systems need governance, access controls, and oversight

Message: “Knowing limitations makes you a smarter buyer and user. The best AI implementations build in guardrails from day one.”

💼 BLOCK 3: Business Impact (18 minutes)

Goal: Connect AI to real business outcomes through emotional and rational appeal

0:50–0:55 — The Anchor: Their Business (5 min)

Slide: “Let’s Make This About You”

Interactive exercise that grounds everything in their reality

Setup: “Before we look at frameworks and case studies, I want to make sure this is relevant to you — not just theory.”

The Question: “Think about your business for a moment. What’s the one process that eats the most time for the least value? The thing your best people complain about. The task everyone dreads.”

Give them space to answer. Listen actively. Write down what they say.

Then, do the math together:

Their Input Calculation
Hours per week on this task ___ hrs
People involved × ___ people
Fully loaded hourly cost (~$50-75 for most roles) × $___
Weeks per year × 52
Annual cost of this problem **= $___**

“So this one problem is costing you roughly $[X] per year. And that’s before we count the opportunity cost — the deals not closed, the customers not served, the strategy not developed.”

The Pivot: “Keep that number in mind. As we go through the next section — the value framework and case studies — I want you to think about what cutting that number in half would mean for your business.”

If they struggle to identify a problem, prompt with: - “Where does your team spend time on repetitive data entry?” - “What information do people ask for that takes too long to find?” - “Where do things slow down waiting for approvals or reviews?” - “What do you wish updated itself automatically?”

Why this works: - Re-engages them after the break with participation - Creates a personal stake in the rest of the session - Gives you a concrete example to reference throughout - The dollar figure makes it tangible and urgent

Message: “This isn’t abstract. You just put a number on one problem. Now let’s talk about how companies are solving problems like this.”

1:00–1:05 — Quick-Hit Case Studies (5 min)

Slide: “Real Results Across Industries”

Three brief cases — one slide each, 90 seconds per story

Case 1: Stitch Fix — Predictive + Perception AI - Company: Online personal styling service, public company (SFIX) - Problem: Scaling personalized fashion recommendations to millions of customers - Solution: Predictive AI analyzes style preferences, purchase history, and fit data; Perception AI processes clothing images to match aesthetics - Result: 80%+ of revenue driven by algorithmic recommendations; stylists 30% more efficient - Source: Company earnings reports, public filings - AI Type: Predictive + Perception (understands style, predicts preference)

Case 2: Klarna — Conversational AI - Company: Global payments provider, $6.7B valuation - Problem: 2.3 million customer service chats monthly overwhelming human agents - Solution: AI assistant handling full customer conversations, integrated with their systems - Result: AI handles 2/3 of all customer chats; equivalent work of 700 agents; resolution time 2 min vs 11 min - Source: Klarna press release, February 2024 - AI Type: Conversational AI (Perception + Generative)

Case 3: JPMorgan COIN — Document AI - Company: JPMorgan Chase, largest US bank - Problem: Lawyers spending 360,000 hours annually reviewing commercial loan agreements - Solution: Contract Intelligence (COIN) platform reads and extracts data from legal documents - Result: Work that took 360,000 human hours now completed in seconds; fewer errors - Source: Bloomberg, company announcements - AI Type: Perception AI (reads, extracts, categorizes)

Pattern: “Each story matched the RIGHT type of AI to a SPECIFIC problem. These aren’t startups — they’re established companies with verifiable results.”

AI is only as good as the data it learns from.

AI Type Data Required Typical Volume Needed

Perception (Text) Documents, text samples Varies by task

Data Readiness Questions: 1. Is the data accessible? (Not trapped in silos or paper) 2. Is the data clean? (Consistent, accurate, complete) 3. Is the data sufficient? (Enough examples to learn from) 4. Is the data labeled? (For supervised learning tasks)

1:13–1:18 — Investment Framework (5 min)

Slide: “What Does AI Actually Cost?”

The truth: AI is more accessible than most people think. You don’t need a Fortune 500 budget.

The AI Investment Ladder:

Stage Investment What You Get Example Solutions
Learn Free Understand what’s possible for your business Strategy session, opportunity identification
Experiment $0–500/month Try existing tools, validate the concept ChatGPT, built-in AI features, free trials
First Win Under $1K Ready-to-deploy solution for a common need FAQ chatbot, basic automation
Custom Build $2K–10K Solution built for your specific workflow Custom chatbot, document Q&A, automated reports
Scale $10K+ Multiple workflows, integrations, AI agents Connected systems, enterprise deployment

Slide: “The Smart Path Forward”

Most companies think AI requires a massive investment. It doesn’t. The smart path:

  1. Start with a conversation — Understand what’s possible for your specific situation (free)
  2. Pick one pain point — Start with a quick win that proves value
  3. See results — Measure the impact before expanding
  4. Grow from there — Add more workflows as you see ROI

Slide: “Build vs. Buy vs. Partner”

Approach Best When Reality Check
Use existing tools Standard use cases Fast but generic; you adapt to the tool, not the other way around
Buy platform Industry solutions exist Vendor lock-in; 6-12 month implementations; expensive customization
Partner to build Custom needs Your domain expertise + AI expertise = solutions that fit your business
Build in-house Core differentiator Requires AI/ML hires ($150K+/year each), infrastructure, ongoing R&D

Key insight: There’s no single right answer. Some companies thrive with off-the-shelf tools. Others need custom builds. The key is matching your approach to your specific situation — your technical capacity, your budget, and how core this is to your business.

Message: “You don’t need a big budget to start. You need a clear problem and an honest assessment of your options.”

Facilitation Notes

The Parking Lot — Managing Tangential Questions

Purpose: Capture valuable questions without derailing the session flow.

Setup: Keep a visible notepad or whiteboard section labeled “Parking Lot” or “Great Questions for Later.”

When to use: - Question is relevant but would take the conversation off-track - Topic will be covered later in the session - Question requires more depth than the current block allows - Multiple questions start stacking up

How to use: 1. Acknowledge the question: “That’s a great question…” 2. Write it down visibly: “Let me capture that…” 3. Set expectation: “We’ll come back to this in Block 4” or “Let’s discuss this after we cover the framework” 4. Actually return to it — check the parking lot during transitions and at the end

Sample phrases: - “I want to give that the attention it deserves — let me park it and we’ll dig in after the break.” - “That’s exactly what we’re covering in 10 minutes. Mind if I save it so we can discuss with more context?” - “Great question. Let me write that down so I don’t forget — I want to make sure we address it.”

At session end: Review any remaining parking lot items. Address briefly or offer to follow up.

Checkpoints — Confirming Understanding

Purpose: Ensure comprehension before building on concepts. Prevents losing them mid-session.

Built-in checkpoint moments:

After Checkpoint Question What You’re Checking
AI Iceberg (0:10) “Does this framing — that GenAI is just the tip — match your experience? Or surprising?” Whether they’ve bought into the core premise
Four AI Types (0:23) “Quick gut check: which of these four feels most relevant to your business right now?” Whether they understand the distinctions
Capability Framework (0:33) “Before we dig into your business — any of these capabilities feel foreign or unclear?” Whether they’re ready for the interactive section
After Break (0:50) “Anything from the first half you want me to clarify before we jump into your business?” Natural reset point; primes them for the interactive anchor
After Case Studies (1:05) “Any of those examples spark ideas about your own situation?” Whether stories are landing and connecting
Before Closing (1:23) “We’ve covered a lot. What’s the one thing that’s stuck with you most?” Whether key messages are landing

If they seem confused: - Don’t push forward — circle back - Use a different example or analogy - Ask what’s unclear rather than re-explaining the same way - It’s okay to skip ahead if a section isn’t resonating

Signs to watch for: - Furrowed brow, looking away - Generic responses (“Yeah, makes sense…”) - Checking phone or watch - Questions that suggest they missed something earlier

Small Audience / Executive 1:1 Format

This session is designed for intimate settings: a CEO and perhaps 1-2 other leaders. Adjust your approach accordingly:

Conversational, Not Presentational: - Treat slides as conversation guides, not scripts - Pause frequently to ask questions and listen - Let their responses shape the direction - Skip slides if the conversation naturally covers the content

Personalization is Key: - Research their company beforehand (website, LinkedIn, news) - Reference their industry throughout - Connect every concept to their specific situation - By the end, they should have 1-2 concrete AI opportunities identified

Reading the Room: - If they’re engaged on a topic, go deeper — don’t rush to the next slide - If they seem skeptical, acknowledge it and address concerns directly - If they’re excited, capture that energy and help them think practically

Key Messaging Throughout

  • Resist the temptation to over-focus on ChatGPT/generative AI
  • Constantly reinforce: “This is just ONE type of AI”
  • Use phrases like “AI that predicts,” “AI that sees,” “AI that creates”
  • Remind them that proven AI often delivers faster, more reliable ROI
  • Return to the iceberg visual as a touchstone
