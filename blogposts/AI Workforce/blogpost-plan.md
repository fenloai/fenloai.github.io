### 6. When AI Agents Are NOT the Right Choice
**Key Message:** Honest assessment of limitations (Fenlo differentiator).

**Callout Box - Be Honest:**

**Skip AI agents if:**
- Your processes aren't well-defined yet (agents amplify chaos)
- You need 100% accuracy with zero room for error
- The task requires genuine human judgment and nuance
- You're a very small team where flexibility matters more than automation
- Your data is scattered, incomplete, or unreliable
- You're looking for a "set it and forget it" solution

**Consider AI agents if:**
- You have repetitive multi-step workflows eating staff time
- The same process runs dozens or hundreds of times monthly
- Tasks involve gathering, organizing, or summarizing information
- You need 24/7 coverage without 24/7 staffing
- Speed matters but occasional human review is acceptable
- You're ready to invest in proper setup and monitoring

**Key Insight Box:**
"We've declined projects where AI agents weren't the right fit. If your business processes are still evolving or your data is messy, you might need organization before automation. Agents work best when they're amplifying solid foundations."

### 7. The Failure Rate Reality
**Key Message:** Understanding why projects fail helps you avoid the same mistakes.

**Statistics on Failures:**
- 40% of agentic AI projects fail due to inadequate foundations (industry research)
- 70-85% of AI projects still fail overall
- >40% of agentic AI projects predicted to be canceled by 2027 (Gartner)
- Only 30% of GenAI experiments reach full scale within 6 months (Deloitte)

**Common Failure Reasons:**
1. **Unclear business value** — No defined success metrics
2. **Poor data quality** — Garbage in, garbage out
3. **Unrealistic expectations** — Expecting fully autonomous operation immediately
4. **Inadequate oversight** — Not building in human checkpoints
5. **Legacy system conflicts** — Old systems can't support modern AI

**What Works Instead:**
- Start with one well-defined workflow
- Build in human review points
- Measure specific outcomes (time saved, errors reduced)
- Iterate based on results
- Scale only after proving value

### 8. What Implementation Looks Like
**Key Message:** It's more involved than chatbots but less than enterprise overhauls.

**Implementation Checklist:**

1. **Workflow Mapping** (1-2 weeks)
   - Document exact steps in target process
   - Identify decision points and exceptions
   - Define success criteria

2. **System Assessment** (3-5 days)
   - What data/apps does the agent need access to?
   - Are APIs available? What integrations required?
   - What security/compliance considerations apply?

3. **Agent Development** (2-4 weeks)
   - Build agent logic and integrations
   - Create human review checkpoints
   - Develop error handling

4. **Testing & Refinement** (1-2 weeks)
   - Run parallel to existing process
   - Compare outputs
   - Refine based on edge cases

5. **Monitored Deployment**
   - Start with subset of volume
   - Increase autonomy as confidence grows
   - Maintain human oversight

**Realistic Timeline:** 4-8 weeks for most SME implementations

**Cost Considerations:**
- More complex than chatbots or simple automation
- ROI typically measured in hours saved and error reduction
- Best suited for high-volume repetitive workflows

### 9. The Bottom Line: Is This Right for Your Business?
**Key Message:** The technology is real, but fit depends on your specific situation.

**Summary Decision Framework:**

**Good Fit Indicators:**
- Clear, documented processes
- High-volume repetitive workflows
- Reliable data sources
- Willingness to monitor and iterate
- Specific problems you want solved

**Poor Fit Indicators:**
- Still figuring out your processes
- Need perfect accuracy every time
- Very small team with flexible roles
- Looking for hands-off solution
- Unclear on what you'd measure

**Final Paragraph:**
AI agents aren't magic. They're practical tools that work well for specific situations. The organizations seeing real ROI are the ones treating this as a capability investment, not a silver bullet. For SMEs with the right foundations, the time and cost savings can be substantial. For those without, there might be groundwork to do first.
